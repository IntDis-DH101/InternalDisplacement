Natural Disasters in Highly Populated Areas

Globally, internal displacement is caused by 2 main factors; conflicts and natural disasters. Concentrating on the effects of natural disasters, East Asia and Pacific stuck out as the most significantly impacted. The East Asia and Pacific region consists of many countries with densely populated urban areas which results in an exponentially higher number of individuals affected by disasters. In comparison to other regions across the globe, the East Asia and Pacific region experiences the most amount of typhoons, floods, earthquakes, tsunamis and volcanic eruptions. (GRID 2020, 39) With the combination of dense populations and likelihood of disasters, this region remains a hotspot, resulting in an average of approximately 12 million internally displaced persons (IDPs) in the past decade. Implementation Capabilities on Internal Displacement


Support from government, Funding Mechanisms, and Institutional Focal Points

This bar graph depicts the implementation indicator scores of East Asia and Pacific countries included in our data set. The implementation indicator is derived from the average of three other indicators; support from government and aid providers, funding mechanisms, and institutional focal point. This visualization underscores the uniformity of these countries' implementation abilities, except Thailand, which scored 0.67. Indonesia, Papua New Guinea, and the Philippines have a score of 1, which indicates the best scenario for implementation practices. Thailand's oversight strategies can elucidate the country's low implementation score. The 2021 Internal Displacement Index report from the Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre (IDMC) highlights the absence of a data collection system for internal displacement in Thailand. The country also does not have a national policy earmarked to address internal displacement despite having a National Disaster Risk Management Plan and a Climate Change Master Plan. There is, however, an entity that provides immediate assistance during disasters; the Ministry of the Inferior's Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation. While necessary, the Ministry's contribution does not address the long-term repercussions of natural disasters. Therefore, we deduced that the displacement levels in Thailand have, to some extent, a direct correlation with the state's policies and capabilities, with the primary cause being climate change.











Preventive and Long-term Measures


Preemptive Evacuations and Local Integration

Many countries are responding to natural hazards through preventive ways as a growing consensus of climate being a cause for displacement is experienced worldwide. One of the most prominent strategies is preemptive evacuations, "with people alerted to leave their homes by text message or phone call" (GRID 2021, 48). However, long-term sustainable local integration is a favored preference among IDPs. Yet, this requires solid local governance, which numerous regions lack, and decentralizing multilateral frameworks. Individuals prefer to relocate locally due to “security considerations, social networks and better living conditions, economic opportunities and public services.” (GRID 2021, 107). To make local integration a successful durable solution, IDPs need to assert their agency and become involved in the decision-making process.
