What the Data Tells Us


Data says

Globally, internal displacement is caused by 2 main factors; conflicts and natural disasters. Concentrating on the effects of natural disasters, East Asia and Pacific stuck out as the most significantly impacted. The East Asia and Pacific region consists of many countries with dense populated urban areas which results in an exponentially higher number of individuals affected by disasters. In comparison to other regions across the globe, the East Asia and Pacific region experiences the most amount of typhoons, floods, earthquakes, tsunamis and volcanic eruptions. (GRID 2020, 39) With the combination of dense populations and likelihood of disasters, this region remains a hotspot, resulting in an average of approximately 12 million internally displaced persons (IPDs) in the past decade.
