South Asia


Conflict, Violence, and Disaster

South Asia is one of the regions that experiences the most natural disasters and also some of the highest numbers of displacement due to disasters. In 2020, the region experienced 9.2 million new disaster displacements out of 9.6 million new displacements due to both conflict and violence and disasters (GRID 2021, 51). A point of analysis that can help reason the high numbers of displacement, besides the natural hazards themselves, are the national policies. This stacked bar graph compares the collection of five different national policies across South Asian countries. Afghanistan and Sri Lanka have the highest collective average at 5, while Pakistan holds the lowest average at 2. Pakistan has scant durable solutions which can be argued to be a product of the inexistence of national policy on internal displacement and few policies that include prevention measures. Evironmental and Implementation Abilities


The Disparity Between High Internal Displacement Policy and Low Environmental Sustainability

This visualization portrays the difference between existing policies and their implementation juxtaposed against environmental sustainability. The five South Asian countries in our data set have values ranging between 0.49 and 0.93 for policies and capacities index. This index is calculated as an arithmetic average of three indicators; data indicator, national policies indicator and implementation indicator. The environmental sustainability numbers vary from 0.25 to 0.39. Environmental sustainability is based on the Environmental Performance Index which considers a country’s environmental challenges such as exposure to natural hazards and the byproducts of that including food insecurity that results from damage of the agricultural sector from natural disasters. For both measures, a value of 0 means the worst case scenario and a value of 1 being the best case scenario. There is a considerable difference in values for each country. A question that arises from our observations is why policies and capacities measures addressing internal displacement have high values and that is not reflected on the environmental sustainability of the countries since the policies in place should better protect from exposures to natural disasters.

The disparity in values according to scholarship (GRID 2021, Internal Displacement Index 2021) is particularly due to recognition of climate disasters and climate change as a push factor for displacement. The region of South Asia, due to its geographical location, has historically been vulnerable to climate disasters and given this, there have been more efforts to help alleviate the repercussions of these events, since disaster displacement became more widely acknowledged at the turn of the century. Yet most of these efforts are in the form of evacuation strategies, resources dependent on fund eligibility and humanitarian aid funds. Eligibility criteria for funding is often based on strong governance and administrative capacities requirements, which countries who are considered fragile do not meet and do not receive funding (GRID 2021, 100). Similarly, the strategies and implementation policies in place are not enough to protect the livelihoods of people and provide durable solutions. For instance, in May 2020, India’s and Bangladesh’s government evacuated about five million people as cyclone Amphan approached their coasts. The evacuation possibly saved thousands of lives yet the exposure to the natural disaster event still caused the destruction of homes and infrastructure that left thousands homeless. (GRID 2021, 53) Moreover, the people who do not receive aid are also severely impacted by climate disasters since despite rapid economic growth in this region, there is rampant inequality, with many people living in poverty. The lack of economic stability makes individuals more vulnerable to climate disasters, particularly as climate disasters occur more often and for longer periods of time and are more severe. In South Asia, monitoring of the monsoon season has shown that “extended monsoon seasons are becoming the norm.” (GRID 2021) and climate change is largely responsible for that.

No matter how many policies and implementation efforts there are, climate disasters are escalating and it is almost impossible for policy making to keep up with climate change and the impact it has on people. In fact, the top three countries that experienced the most internal displacement in 2020 were South Asian countries; Afghanistan, India and Pakistan (GRID 2021; Internal displacement in a changing climate).
